About Us

Peacock is the national bird of India. They are pretty large birds with the ability to fly a short distance. Their body can weigh up to 5 to 6 kg, while their length from the tip of the beak to the end of the train is 190 to 230 cm. The head, neck and breast of the peacock are of royal blue colour.The color of the peacock is dark greenish-brown and blue. A peacock has beautiful and colorful feathers. It has dots like the moon on its long feathers. The peacock has two legs, two eyes, a long and thin neck, a beak, a beautiful crest and long tail feathers. Most noteworthy, the bird is famous for its beautiful vibrant colours. The Peacock is popular for its spectacular beauty. It certainly has a hypnotic appearance. Watching it dance during the Monsoon season is a great pleasure experience.

Peacocks are non-fussy omnivores and will eat most plant foods such as seeds, grains, berries, vegetables and fruits as well as insects, arthropods, small lizards, snakes, frogs and rodents. They require a high-protein diet to keep their feathers healthy and generally have strong appetites.The Indian peacock (Pavo cristatus) has iridescent blue and green plumage, mostly metal-like blue and green. In both species, females are a little smaller than males in terms of weight and wingspan, but males are significantly longer due to the "tail", also known as a "train".

There are two familiar peacock species. The blue peacock lives in India and Sri Lanka, while the green peacock is found in Java and Myanmar (Burma). A more distinct and little-known species, the Congo peacock, inhabits African rain forests.

One of the most impressive scenes in the world of birds is a peacock displaying its impressive, iridescent feathers, technically known as its train
